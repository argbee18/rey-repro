No. 01

Nigiri Sushi

握り寿司

Hand-pressed vinegared rice crowned with a slice of pristine fish. The balance of acidity, sweetness, and salt in the rice is everything — the fish rests on a foundation, not the other way around.

Key Ingredients

  • Sushi rice
  • Rice vinegar
  • Salmon/Tuna
  • Wasabi
  • Soy sauce
  • Nori

Method

  1. Cook short-grain rice; season with vinegar, sugar, and salt while fanning to cool.
  2. Slice fish on a bias into 5mm-thick pieces against the grain.
  3. Wet hands, shape a small oval of rice, dab wasabi, and press fish firmly on top.
Noodle · Broth 3 hrs

No. 02

Tonkotsu Ramen

豚骨ラーメン

A milky, collagen-rich pork-bone broth so thick it barely clings to the bowl. This Fukuoka icon takes hours, but the depth of flavor is unlike anything out of a packet.

Key Ingredients

  • Pork bones
  • Thin noodles
  • Chashu pork
  • Soft-boiled egg
  • Nori
  • Green onion

Method

  1. Blanch pork bones, rinse, then simmer at a rolling boil for 3 hours until broth turns opaque white.
  2. Season broth with tare (salt and soy blend) to taste.
  3. Cook noodles, ladle broth over, and finish with chashu, marinated egg, and nori.
Fried · Light 25 min

No. 03

Ebi Tempura

海老天ぷら

Shrimp encased in a shatteringly light, lacy batter — the opposite of heavy Western deep-fry. Cold water and minimal mixing are the secrets to keeping it airy.

Key Ingredients

  • Tiger shrimp
  • Cake flour
  • Ice-cold water
  • Egg yolk
  • Neutral oil
  • Dashi dipping sauce

Method

  1. Score shrimp belly to prevent curling; pat very dry.
  2. Mix icy cold water with egg yolk and sifted flour in 3 strokes — lumps are fine.
  3. Dip shrimp and fry at 180 °C for 2 minutes until barely golden; drain on a rack.
Soup · Daily 10 min

No. 04

Miso Shiru

味噌汁

Japan's quiet daily ritual — a gentle umami broth of dashi and fermented soybean paste. Every household has a signature version, and every version is correct.

Key Ingredients

  • White miso
  • Kombu
  • Katsuobushi
  • Silken tofu
  • Wakame
  • Green onion

Method

  1. Steep kombu in cold water, heat to just before boiling, remove and add katsuobushi for 3 minutes to make dashi.
  2. Dissolve miso in a ladle of dashi, then stir back into the pot — never boil after adding miso.
  3. Add tofu cubes and wakame; serve immediately topped with sliced green onion.
Grilled · Glazed 30 min

No. 05

Teriyaki Chicken

照り焼きチキン

Chicken thighs lacquered in a glossy reduction of soy, mirin, and sake. The word "teriyaki" comes from the shining glaze (teri) and the grilling technique (yaki) — it says everything about the dish.

Key Ingredients

  • Chicken thighs
  • Soy sauce
  • Mirin
  • Sake
  • Sugar
  • Sesame seeds

Method

  1. Sear skin-side chicken thighs in a dry pan until deeply golden; flip and cook through.
  2. Pour away fat; add equal parts soy, mirin, and sake plus sugar and reduce until syrupy.
  3. Coat chicken in the glaze and rest 2 minutes before slicing.
Dumpling · Pan-fried 40 min

No. 06

Yaki Gyoza

焼き餃子

Crisp-bottomed, steamed-top dumplings filled with pork and cabbage. The iconic ridged fold is beautiful, but a simple half-moon pleat produces the same crunch and taste.

Key Ingredients

  • Gyoza wrappers
  • Ground pork
  • Napa cabbage
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Sesame oil

Method

  1. Salt and squeeze cabbage dry; mix with pork, garlic, ginger, soy, and sesame oil.
  2. Fill wrappers, pleat edges to seal, and place flat-side down in a hot oiled pan.
  3. Fry until golden, add ¼ cup water, cover and steam 3 minutes, then uncover to crisp.
Street Food · Osaka 30 min

No. 07

Takoyaki

たこ焼き

Osaka's beloved street balls — a dashi-infused batter hiding tender octopus, turned continuously in a special molded pan to achieve a crisp shell and molten, custardy center.

Key Ingredients

  • Octopus
  • Dashi batter
  • Tenkasu
  • Pickled ginger
  • Takoyaki sauce
  • Bonito flakes

Method

  1. Make a thin batter with flour, dashi, soy, and egg; heat the takoyaki pan and oil each mold.
  2. Fill molds with batter, add octopus, tenkasu, and ginger; pour batter to overflow slightly.
  3. Use skewers to rotate each ball every 90 seconds until fully round and golden all over.
Noodle · Comfort 20 min

No. 08

Kake Udon

かけうどん

Thick, chewy wheat noodles afloat in a crystal-clear dashi broth. Deceptively simple, the quality rests entirely on the noodles and the broth — neither can hide.

Key Ingredients

  • Udon noodles
  • Kombu dashi
  • Light soy sauce
  • Mirin
  • Fish cake
  • Spring onion

Method

  1. Combine dashi with light soy sauce and mirin; taste — the broth should be delicate and golden.
  2. Cook fresh or frozen udon in boiling water until just tender; rinse and drain.
  3. Place noodles in a bowl, pour hot broth over, and top with fish cake and sliced spring onion.
Rice · Portable 15 min

No. 09

Onigiri

おにぎり

Japan's original portable meal — a triangle of seasoned rice wrapped in nori with a surprise filling inside. Every convenience store sells them; none match the ones made by hand at home.

Key Ingredients

  • Japanese rice
  • Nori sheets
  • Sea salt
  • Tuna mayo
  • Salmon flakes
  • Umeboshi

Method

  1. Salt wet hands; take a handful of warm cooked rice, press a well in the center and add filling.
  2. Close the rice around the filling and press firmly into a triangle shape, rotating to compact evenly.
  3. Wrap the bottom third with a strip of nori just before eating to keep it crisp.
Fried · Hearty 40 min

No. 10

Katsu Curry

カツカレー

A golden panko-crusted pork cutlet resting on steamed rice, flooded with a thick, mildly spiced Japanese curry sauce. Rich, filling, and deeply satisfying — Japan's most-loved comfort food.

Key Ingredients

  • Pork loin
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Curry roux
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Potato

Method

  1. Pound pork to even thickness; coat in flour, egg, then panko; deep-fry at 170 °C until deep golden.
  2. Sauté onion, carrot, and potato until soft; add water and simmer 15 minutes until tender.
  3. Melt curry roux blocks into the vegetables, thicken, and serve alongside sliced katsu on rice.
